About

I work where large language models meet automated program analysis.

I'm a PhD researcher and Graduate Assistant in the Department of Computer and Information Sciences at Clemson University. My work sits at the intersection of large language models, reinforcement learning, and multi-agent systems — building systems that can edit, reason about, and improve code without forgetting what they already know.

Research focus
01

LLMs & model editing

Lifelong, continual editing of large language models that injects new knowledge without catastrophic forgetting of what the model already encodes.

02

Multi-agent systems

Coordinating concurrent agents so they can divide, parallelize, and safely merge work across large automated program-analysis tasks.

03

RL for program analysis

Reinforcement-learning methods that let agents explore, analyze, and reason about real codebases automatically.
